About

I grew up near the shore of a bay much like that pictured above. That probably explains my immediate connection to the video it was captured from. Or maybe after almost 30 years of involvement in systems administration I'm just a sucker for the sense of serenity it conveys.

Most of my career as a tech professional has been as a sysadmin ("I am root"). For the 15 years starting at the beginning of this century I served as lead engineer for directory services ("I am Directory Manager") at a large global company. Over the years I've wrestled with refrigerator-sized servers, tried to make sense of the "camel put together by a committee" that is a certain enterprise resource planning application stack, written a pile of perl code, and fought off potentially crippling DDOS attacks coming from purportedly "secure" networks.

As more and more software solutions have moved off-premesis to the cloud, I've been spending a lot of time working with the new technologies that are being made possible by application containers, software defined networking and serverless architectures. It's been a wild ride, and it looks like I just won an E-ticket to another round.
